# Role Structure Guide (PM, TL, Specialist Roles)

Use this guide when generating or upgrading role skills.

## Required Role Skeleton

1. frontmatter with strong trigger description
2. mission and decision mandate
3. operating model (how role works)
4. playbooks and anti-patterns
5. deliverables and templates
6. validation and blocked-state behavior
7. runtime boundary

## Product Manager Role Expectations

- lock problem framing and BRD scope
- define non-goals and release criteria
- prioritize by impact, effort, confidence
- provide stakeholder-ready release briefs

## Tech Lead Role Expectations

- convert BRD into feature roadmap (now/next/later)
- define architecture decision records and boundaries
- coordinate multi-agent lanes with ownership clarity
- enforce quality gates, rollback readiness, and risk tracking

## Specialist Role Expectations

- explicit capability boundary
- deterministic workflow where possible
- reusable templates/scripts for repeated tasks
- measurable output contract

## Depth Checks

A role skill is likely too shallow if:
- it only lists generic bullet points
- it has no reusable templates
- it defines no decision rights
- it cannot be validated in practice
